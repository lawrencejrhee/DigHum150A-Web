About me

Who I am and what I do

I'm an EECS student at UC Berkeley, graduating in Fall 2026. I grew up in San Jose, so tech was just kind of around me growing up. Most of my time at Berkeley has gone into hardware, chips, circuits, that kind of thing. I've always liked the humanities too, and this class is basically an excuse to actually do something with that side of me.

My journey

A lot of my coursework has been about chip design, all the way through tapeout, which is when a design actually gets manufactured and there's no more room for mistakes. It's stressful, exacting work and I like it, but I've never wanted to only think about engineering. I'm curious about memory and who gets to decide what's worth keeping, and that's basically why I signed up for DigHum 150A. I don't think caring about archives and caring about circuits are that different, honestly.
