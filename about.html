About me

Who I am and what I do

I'm an EECS student at UC Berkeley, graduating in Fall 2026. I grew up in San Jose, inside the same tech economy I now build chips for, and most of my four years here have gone toward hardware: processors, circuits, the kind of work that ends in a piece of silicon instead of a sentence. I also care about the humanities, and this class is where that side of me gets to do something.

My journey

Tapeout is the moment a chip design stops being a file and becomes a physical object, the last checkpoint before a mistake turns permanent. That's the kind of exacting, high-stakes work I've spent most of my time on at Berkeley, and I like it. But I've never wanted my thinking to stop at engineering. I'm curious about memory and about who decides what's worth keeping, and DigHum 150A is where that curiosity gets to run. Pointing the same care I bring to a circuit at an archive isn't a stretch. It's the same instinct, aimed somewhere else.
