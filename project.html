Final Project In development

My final project for DigHum 150A will be a digital mini-exhibit and the beginnings of a digital archive, built with the Omeka publishing platform. It will combine primary-source items, descriptive metadata, and a narrative interpretation written for both scholarly and public audiences.

I haven't locked in a topic yet. That's what Week 2's blog post is for, so this page is intentionally bare right now. Once I've picked what the archive is going to be built around, this section turns into the real pitch.

What to expect

The archive

A curated collection of items, each described with a consistent metadata scheme.

The exhibit

A multimedia narrative that interprets the collection and invites visitors to explore it.

The user guide

A plain-language guide explaining how to navigate the archive and the reasoning behind my curatorial decisions.
