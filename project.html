Final Project In development

My final project for DigHum 150A is going to be a digital mini-exhibit and the start of a digital archive, built with the Omeka publishing platform. It'll combine actual items, metadata, and some written interpretation, aimed at both a scholarly and a public audience.

I haven't picked a topic yet, that's what next week's blog post is supposed to force me to do. I'll fill this section in once I actually know what the archive's about.

What to expect

The archive

A curated collection of items, each described with a consistent metadata scheme.

The exhibit

A multimedia narrative that interprets the collection and invites visitors to explore it.

The user guide

A plain-language guide explaining how to navigate the archive and the reasoning behind my curatorial decisions.

A link to the live exhibit will appear here in August 2026.
